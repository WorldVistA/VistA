What's New in GUI v.30.b (OR*3.0*350)

  • Clinic Medications - CPRS has broadened its ability to order medications to be administered in clinic settings.

  • Clinic Infusions - CPRS has broadened it ability to order infusion medications to be administered in clinic.

  • One-Step Clinic Administration for IV Medications - This feature allows providers to order, administer, and document the administration of intravenous medications in clinic. This feature is only used when the physicians administer the medication themselves in clinic.

  • One-Step Clinic Unit Dose Medication Administration - This feature allows providers to order, administer, and document the administration of unit dose medications in clinic. This feature is only used when the physicians administer the medication themselves in clinic.

  • Clinically Indicated Date - For consults, the term Earliest Appopriate Date has been replaced by the term Clinically Indicated Date. This change is in several locations in CPRS.

  • Display of Laboratory Test results - There have been changes to the display of Lab test results.

  • Ordering Supplies

    • Entering Simple Supply Orders

    • Entering Complex Supply Orders

  • New NonRenewable RX Request for... Notification - For sites that use AudioRenewal and want to have an alert for when a non-renewable prescription can be sent to a provider. If the provider wants to continue the medication therapy, the notification or alert will take them provider to the Orders tab where they can use the Copy to New Order feature to renew the prescription.

  • Diet Orders - When a clinician enters a diet order for a patient, CPRS now displays active and delayed diets to help avoid a conflict. Users should review the diet orders and act appropriately based on the information.

  • Release of Hold Orders - Orders that have expired cannot be released. Before there was a problem where the order status could be confusing.
